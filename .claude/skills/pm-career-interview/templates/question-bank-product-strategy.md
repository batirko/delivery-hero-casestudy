# Question Bank: Product Strategy (88 questions)

**Source:** Aakash Gupta's Product Strategy Cheat Sheet (Top 88 Practice Questions).

Strategy questions don't have a single framework — they test structured thinking under ambiguity. Common shape: **clarify the question → define success → analyze the options → recommend one → defend it**.

Useful lenses to pull from:
- **3C** — Customer, Competitor, Company
- **Porter's 5 Forces** — supplier power, buyer power, rivalry, new entrants, substitutes
- **Build/Buy/Partner** — for any new capability
- **Offensive vs Defensive** — are we attacking or protecting?
- **Blue Ocean** — new market creation vs red ocean competition

## Drill Tips for Strategy

- **Always define success first.** "Before I recommend, let me state the goal: [X metric] over [Y timeframe]."
- **Name trade-offs explicitly.** "Option A gets us fast growth but burns cash. Option B is slower but defensible."
- **Commit to one answer.** Hedging is the #1 mistake. "I'd do A" always beats "it depends."
- **Give a measurement plan.** Strategy without metrics is vibes.
- **Know 2-3 recent M&A or strategic moves** for each target company. Interviewers love when you cite specifics.
