# Question Bank: Product Design

**Framework:** CIRCLES — see `frameworks.md`.

Product design is the **most frequent** PM interview type (30-40% of rounds). Treat it as the primary drill.

Pulled from Aakash Gupta's cheat sheets and the Product Leadership set. Mix with live company products when prepping for a specific loop.

## "Design a product for X" (greenfield)

2. How would you approach designing an educational app for children?
3. Design a product to help people find apartments.
4. Design an app for a grocery store.
5. Design an algorithm for a self-driving car (use design lens, not strict CS).
6. Design a high-speed network to communicate with team members on Mars.
7. Design a fitness app for kids.
8. Create a new Siri — what would you do differently?
9. Redesign the city public park experience.
10. Bootstrap a product that helps people find apartments.
11. What sets of capabilities do you put in your strategy and design for a consumer restaurant / search app?
12. What's the first product you would ship if you were a PM at a startup working with NHL big data?

## Questions that often show up with the "I'll know it when I hear it" test

These sound like design questions but interviewers are really probing for:

- **Taste** — do you have a point of view on what good looks like?
- **Prioritization** — can you cut?
- **User obsession** — do you name a real persona or hand-wave?

Red-flag answers:
- "Add a search bar" as a solution (be specific about what they're searching for)
- "Increase engagement" as a goal (define which metric)
- "Users want X" with no segmentation
- 4+ solutions, no recommendation

## Company-specific design prompts

Before every interview, prep one "how I'd improve [their product]" answer. Go deep: identify an actual weak flow, propose a solution, name the metric you'd move, state a guardrail. This often becomes your interviewer's favorite answer if it shows real product usage.

For Meta: Instagram Stories, Reels engagement, or a Messenger monetization angle.
For Google: Maps discovery, Photos search, or a Search ads UX tradeoff.
For Amazon: a specific marketplace friction (returns, 3P quality, search relevance).
For Apple: a cross-product gap (Health ↔ Fitness, Notes ↔ Reminders, etc.).
For Stripe/Square/fintech: developer experience, checkout conversion, or dispute flow.
