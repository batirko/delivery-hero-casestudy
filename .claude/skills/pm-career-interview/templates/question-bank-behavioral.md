# Question Bank: Behavioral (84 questions)

**Source:** Aakash Gupta's PM Behavioral Interview Cheat Sheet (Top 84 Practice Questions). Framework: **DIGS** or STAR — see `frameworks.md`.

When drilling: mix categories. Real interviews don't stay in one lane.

---

## Most Common (always get asked)

1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why do you want to work here?
3. Why this job?
4. Why are you leaving your current job?
5. What is your greatest weakness?
6. What is your biggest failure?
7. Fast forward three years. What will you be then?
8. What's something everyone takes for granted that you think is hogwash?
9. What work accomplishment are you most proud of?
10. Describe a scenario which required you to say no to an idea or project.
11. What is your current pay?
12. What are your compensation expectations?

> **Note on comp questions:** In many jurisdictions (CA, NY, etc.), asking current pay is illegal. Know your region.

## Personal Favorites (wildcards)

Everyone has a wildcard. These surface values, self-awareness, and chemistry.

1. To what do you attribute your success?
2. What is the hardest thing you've ever done?
3. Describe a time you were involved in a controversial product decision.
4. Tell me what work you are most proud of.
5. Tell me about how you navigated an ambiguous situation.
6. What unfair secrets have you heard to improve the velocity of your product team?
7. What would your siblings say about you?
8. Tell me about a time something went wrong.
9. Tell me something you did that was a good decision and why it didn't work.
10. Tell me something you have learned about yourself that has revealed a limitation about how you work.
11. Do you consider yourself lucky?
12. Tell me about a time that you delivered something that was impactful.

## Product-Related (PM-specific skills)

1. Pick a product you love. What makes it stand out? How could it improve?
2. As a PM, how do you balance user needs and business goals?
3. Describe a change you'd make to our product and why.
4. Share an instance where data swayed a critical stakeholder.
5. How do you prioritize features under tight timelines?
6. What's the most rewarding aspect of Product Management for you?
7. Describe your decision-making process for feature development.
8. How do you ensure a user-friendly interface in your products?
9. Propose a feature enhancement for a commonly used app and its potential impact.
10. In your view, what makes a product truly successful?
11. Share an example of resolving a significant product challenge or receiving harsh feedback.
12. How do you balance innovation with practicality, like bugs?

## Specific (company + product knowledge)

1. If given a chance, how would you redesign our flagship product?
2. Describe your strategy for managing products aimed at both B2B and B2C markets.
3. What's a key improvement you'd implement for our product soon?
4. Forecast a major challenge our company might face soon.
5. How do you balance product innovation with market demands?
6. In your opinion, what sets our product apart from competitors?
7. Describe a typical day for a Product Manager in our industry.
8. How do you measure the success of a newly implemented feature?
9. What strategies do you use to keep your team motivated and focused?
10. Describe a product failure you experienced and what you learned from it.
11. How do you approach setting and managing customer expectations?
12. Share an example of how you've adapted to significant industry changes.

## True Behavioral (generic, any industry)

1. Describe a recent workplace conflict and how you resolved it.
2. What's your ultimate career aspiration?
3. How do you approach giving constructive feedback? Can you share an example?
4. Describe a significant challenge you faced and overcame.
5. What's your strategy for engaging with customers or users?
6. Can you give an example of using data to drive a key decision?
7. How do you handle the pressure of tight deadlines?
8. Share an experience of adapting quickly in response to changing work priorities.
9. How do you manage work-life balance while meeting high performance standards?
10. Describe a time when you had to lead a team through a difficult situation.
11. How do you handle receiving critical feedback?
12. Share an experience where you had to make a tough decision without complete data.

## Analytical (quant instincts)

1. Estimate the current online population in Southeast Asia.
2. How many doors might there be in Washington D.C.?
3. What's the annual sales figure for AirPods in the USA?
4. Approximate the yearly expenditure on gas in Europe.
5. What approach would you take to estimate the number of houses in North Carolina?
6. Faced with declining metrics, what steps would you take to identify and address the root cause?
7. How would you estimate the market size for a new tech product in an emerging market?
8. Calculate the potential impact of a 10% price increase on a popular software product.
9. How would you approach estimating the user base for a niche mobile app?
10. Devise a method to track the effectiveness of a recent marketing campaign.
11. Propose a strategy for evaluating customer satisfaction in a service industry.
12. How would you determine the optimal inventory level for a seasonal product?

## Technical (PM ↔ Eng)

1. What's your take on Agile?
2. How do you integrate technical teams into the product vision effectively?
3. Can you share an experience where a technical concept became a commercial success?
4. How do you bridge the gap between market-oriented teams and technical challenges?
5. Describe a successful cross-functional collaboration you were part of.
6. How do you prioritize user experience in the design process?
7. Describe a situation where you had to advocate for the user's needs against business pressures.
8. What techniques do you use to stay ahead of industry trends?
9. How do you foster a culture of continuous improvement within your team?
10. How do you think Product Managers interact with engineers?
11. What are the key technical challenges in developing a new product?
12. How do you align engineering goals with market demands?

## The Story Bank — What to Prep

You can't prep 84 answers. Prep 7 stories that map to 80% of questions. See `frameworks.md` → The Story Bank section.

| Story | Maps to questions about |
|---|---|
| The high-stakes launch | pressure, execution, cross-functional |
| The hard no | leadership, saying no, influence |
| The failed bet | failure, learning, resilience |
| The tough teammate | conflict, EQ, feedback |
| The data surprise | data-driven, pivot, analysis |
| The user insight | empathy, strategic change |
| The proud moment | impact, purpose |

For each: prep 60s, 2-min, and 5-min versions.
